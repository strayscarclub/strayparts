About Stray Parts

Built to help enthusiasts find rare parts and keep older cars alive.

Why I built Stray Parts

Stray Parts was built with a simple idea in mind: help keep older cars on the road.

A lot of the cars enthusiasts care most about are getting older. The parts are not always easy to find, the good ones do not appear every day, and keeping these cars alive often comes down to the people who care enough to keep searching, learning, fixing and preserving them.

I wanted to build something that could genuinely help with that.

Stray Parts brings together multiple marketplaces in one place, so enthusiasts can search more easily, save the listings they care about, and set alerts for the parts they have been waiting on.

Keeping older cars alive

This project is really about giving older cars a better chance of staying on the road.

Every old car that is still running, still being restored, or still being improved usually has someone behind it putting in the time and effort to keep it going. That side of car culture matters to me. It is not just about buying parts. It is about preserving cars that would otherwise get harder and harder to maintain.

If Stray Parts can make that process even a little easier, then it is doing what it was built to do.

Whether it is a long-term restoration, a weekend project, or a car someone has owned for years, the goal is the same: help enthusiasts keep building, maintaining and saving the cars they care about.

I’m an enthusiast too

Stray Parts was built from being part of this world and wanting to contribute something useful to it.

I care about older cars, the culture around them, and the people who keep them alive. That is why I want Stray Parts to feel simple, honest and genuinely helpful.

Not overdone. Not full of noise. Just a useful tool built for enthusiasts who know what these cars mean and why they are worth keeping on the road.

The mission

The mission is simple: help enthusiasts keep older cars alive by making rare parts easier to find across multiple marketplaces.

Stray Parts is still growing, but that is the direction behind it from the start and the reason it exists now.
