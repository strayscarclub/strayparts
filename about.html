About Stray Parts

Built to help enthusiasts find rare parts and keep older cars alive.

Why I built Stray Parts

Stray Parts came from the same frustration a lot of enthusiasts know well. Finding the right part for an older car often means jumping between multiple marketplaces, digging through vague listings, translating Japanese pages, and hoping the part has not already sold by the time you find it.

I wanted one place that made that process easier. Something simple that helps people search across multiple sources, keep track of the parts they care about, and stay ready when something rare finally appears.

Keeping older cars alive

A big part of this project is about helping enthusiasts keep older cars on the road. The longer I have been around old cars, the more I have realised how much time gets lost just trying to find the right parts. Sometimes that search is half the battle.

Stray Parts is my way of making that easier. Whether someone is restoring a car, keeping a long-term project going, or chasing a part they have wanted for years, the goal is the same: make the hunt simpler and give those cars a better chance of staying alive.

I’m an enthusiast too

This site was not built from the outside looking in. I am into older cars myself, and that is a big reason Stray Parts exists in the first place. I know what it feels like to spend hours searching, comparing listings, translating descriptions and trying not to miss the good stuff.

That is also why I want Stray Parts to feel useful, honest and focused on what actually matters. Less clutter, better searches, and a better chance of finding the parts that are getting harder to track down.

The mission

The mission is simple: help enthusiasts find rare parts faster, make Japanese marketplace searching easier, and do a small part in helping older cars stay on the road for longer.

Stray Parts is still growing, but that is the direction behind it from the start.
